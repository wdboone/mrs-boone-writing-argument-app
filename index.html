1. What is a CLAIM in an argument essay?

Answer: A claim is the main argument or position you are taking on a topicâ€”it's what you're trying to convince the reader to believe.

2. Name three types of evidence you can use to support a claim.

Answer: Facts, statistics, expert opinions, examples, and anecdotes (any three).

3. What is the difference between evidence and reasoning?

Answer: Evidence is the data or information that supports your claim. Reasoning is your EXPLANATION of how and why that evidence connects to your claim.

4. Why should you include a counterclaim in your argument?

Answer: Including a counterclaim shows you've considered other viewpoints, builds your credibility, and gives you a chance to prove your position is still stronger through a rebuttal.

5. What is a REBUTTAL?

Answer: A rebuttal is your response to the counterclaim, where you explain why the opposing argument is flawed or why your position is still stronger.

6. What are the three parts of the C-E-R model?

Answer: C = Claim (your argument), E = Evidence (facts/data), R = Reasoning (your explanation of why the evidence matters).

7. Name two types of hooks you could use in your introduction.

Answer: Any two of: surprising fact, rhetorical question, bold statement, vivid description, quotation.

8. What is a logical fallacy? Give one example.

Answer: A logical fallacy is an error in reasoning that weakens your argument. Examples include: ad hominem, straw man, bandwagon, false dilemma, slippery slope, or hasty generalization.

9. What do ETHOS, PATHOS, and LOGOS mean?

Answer: Ethos = appeal to credibility/trust, Pathos = appeal to emotions, Logos = appeal to logic and reason.

10. True or False: It's okay to introduce new evidence in your conclusion.

Answer: FALSE! Never introduce new evidence in your conclusion. It's for summarizing and closing.
